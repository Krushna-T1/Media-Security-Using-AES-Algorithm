Why AES?

Strong Security:
AES uses a symmetric key encryption algorithm, which means that the same key is used for both encryption and decryption. This makes it highly secure, as the key can only be known by authorized parties.

Speed:
AES is designed to be fast and efficient, making it a popular choice for encrypting large amounts of data.

Compatibility:
AES is widely supported by a range of software and hardware platforms, making it easy to integrate into existing systems.

Scalability:
AES can be implemented in a variety of key sizes, including 128-bit, 192-bit, and 256-bit, which allows for flexibility in balancing security needs with performance requirements.

Resistance to attacks:
AES has been extensively tested and is resistant to a variety of attacks, including brute force, differential and linear cryptanalysis, and more.

Standardization:
AES is a widely recognized and accepted encryption standard, which means that it is trusted and used by many organizations and governments around the world.
